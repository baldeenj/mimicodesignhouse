*Role: Lead UX & Service Designer — Ontario Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development (MLITSD).*

## Overview

The Ontario Ministry of Labour modernized its primary digital hub for health and safety training and certification. The redesign addressed a fragmented, text-heavy platform that was difficult to navigate — especially for users with accessibility needs or limited digital literacy.

## The Challenge

- A complex information hierarchy with hundreds of documents scattered across disconnected pages
- Legacy templates failing WCAG 2.1 AA and AODA accessibility standards
- Three distinct audiences — employers, workers, and training providers — sharing a single portal
- Dense legal language that reduced comprehension
- Limited analytics on how people actually navigated and searched

## Our Approach

**Discovery** — Stakeholder interviews, moderated user testing, search-log analysis, and persona development.

**Information architecture** — Reorganized around user top-tasks ("Get Certified," "Find a Course") rather than ministry structure, with service blueprints mapping user actions to the backend processes behind them.

**Accessibility** — Applied WCAG 2.1 AA across every template, converted PDFs into accessible HTML, and validated with assistive technologies (JAWS, NVDA, WAVE).

**Content strategy** — Translated legal requirements into plain language, consolidated duplicate content, and established governance guidelines to keep it that way.

**Design & delivery** — Built responsive prototypes in Figma and Adobe XD, maintaining accessibility throughout Agile sprints.

## Outcomes

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance across all redesigned templates
- 45% reduction in time-to-task
- 30%+ increase in successful form submissions and course lookups
- 200+ legacy pages consolidated into a task-oriented architecture
- A content governance framework since adopted across other Ministry sites
