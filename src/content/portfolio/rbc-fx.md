*Role: Senior User Experience Designer — RBC Royal Bank, Commercial Business Banking.*

## Overview

This initiative integrated real-time foreign exchange rates and cross-border payment capabilities into RBC Express, unifying fragmented legacy systems into a single, secure platform for corporate treasury and finance teams managing international transactions.

<figure>
  <img src="/uploads/rbc-fx-1.png" alt="RBC Express foreign exchange view with real-time rates." />
  <figcaption>Real-time FX rates brought directly into RBC Express — no more jumping between portals and external sources.</figcaption>
</figure>

## The Challenge

- Workflows scattered across multiple portals, each with separate authentication
- No real-time FX rate visibility, forcing reliance on external sources
- Complex, form-heavy interfaces incompatible with mobile contexts
- Misalignment with enterprise design standards and accessibility requirements

<figure>
  <img src="/uploads/rbc-fx-2.png" alt="Cross-border payment setup screen in RBC Express." />
  <figcaption>A consolidated cross-border payment flow, replacing several disconnected legacy tools.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Our Approach

Led end-to-end design from discovery through delivery: stakeholder workshops with compliance and trading teams, workflow analysis with corporate users, integrated service blueprinting, responsive prototyping in Figma and Axure, WCAG 2.1 AA verification, and iterative usability testing with financial-operations teams.

## Outcomes

- Consolidated multiple FX workflows into one experience inside RBC Express
- Reduced transaction completion time by ~**25%**
- Boosted confidence through real-time rates and clear confirmations
- Achieved full accessibility compliance and design-system alignment
- Drove measurable adoption of international payment services
