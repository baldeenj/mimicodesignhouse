*Role: Senior User Experience Designer — RBC Royal Bank, Commercial Banking.*

## Overview

Redesigned the password-recovery flow for RBC Express, the online commercial banking platform serving corporate clients — modernizing an outdated, non-responsive experience into a streamlined, human-centered, accessible flow.

<figure>
  <img src="/uploads/rbc-fp-1d.png" alt="Redesigned RBC Express forgot-password start screen on desktop." />
  <figcaption>The redesigned recovery flow — clear language and a mobile-first layout replace the legacy screens.</figcaption>
</figure>

## The Challenge

- A non-responsive interface that was hard to use on mobile
- Overly complex, jargon-heavy language that overwhelmed users
- Inconsistent design and behavior across the platform
- Accessibility gaps failing WCAG standards
- A high volume of password-recovery support calls

## Our Approach

**Discovery** — Heuristic analysis, help-desk data review, and benchmarking against financial-services identity-recovery best practices.

**Optimization** — Mapped the end-to-end journey and simplified a mobile-first flow from six steps to three, with real-time validation and progress indicators.

<figure>
  <img src="/uploads/rbc-fp-3d.png" alt="Verification step of the recovery flow on desktop." />
  <figcaption>Real-time validation and progress indicators keep users oriented through every step.</figcaption>
</figure>

**Accessibility** — WCAG 2.1 AA throughout: contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen-reader support, tested with assistive-technology users.

**Prototyping & testing** — Responsive prototypes in Figma and Axure, moderated usability sessions across devices, and annotated specs for the development team.

## Outcomes

- **40%** reduction in credential-reset time
- **30%** decrease in support tickets within three months
- Full responsive compatibility across mobile and tablet
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance achieved
- New components contributed to RBC's design system
