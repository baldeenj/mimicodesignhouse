## Overview

A data-driven forecasting platform designed to help Air Canada's Network Planning and Revenue Management teams predict passenger demand. The tool transforms complex econometric models into an accessible decision-support system for analysts, planners, and executives.

## The Challenge

The organization struggled to bridge the gap between powerful statistical forecasting models and usable interfaces for non-technical users. Key obstacles included:

- Converting econometric outputs into understandable visuals
- Managing data density without sacrificing usability
- Enabling scenario modeling without requiring technical expertise
- Building user trust through transparency about forecast drivers

## Approach

- Conducted contextual research with data scientists, analysts, and planners
- Applied WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards throughout
- Designed interactive dashboards with adjustable variables (fuel costs, GDP, exchange rates)
- Integrated explainable AI patterns and "insight cards" translating model outputs into plain language
- Created confidence bands and scenario comparison visualizations
- Developed within Agile workflows using Azure DevOps

## Outcomes

- Reduced forecast review time by **40%** through interactive exploration
- Increased non-technical adoption by **35%**
- Achieved full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Enhanced cross-departmental scenario planning and collaboration
