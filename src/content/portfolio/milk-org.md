*Role: Senior Information Architect (agency: No Fixed Address) — Dairy Farmers of Ontario.*

## Overview

Milk.org needed a comprehensive redesign to modernize its digital presence and better serve consumers, farmers, and industry stakeholders. The work strengthened information architecture, improved accessibility, and shaped a clearer brand narrative celebrating Ontario dairy — with intuitive content pathways for each audience.

## The Challenge

- Fragmented experience with buried content and inconsistent navigation
- Limited accessibility compliance and poor mobile usability
- Three distinct audiences (consumers, farmers, industry) with different information needs
- Structural and content foundations that had to be rebuilt from scratch

## Our Approach

**Discovery & research** — Stakeholder workshops plus analysis of site analytics, user feedback, and competitor benchmarks to surface high-value journeys and pain points.

**Information architecture** — A new site map and navigation framework, a taxonomy and metadata model for recipes and educational content, and a content hierarchy aligned to AODA / WCAG 2.1.

  <figcaption>Educational content, restructured — a clean taxonomy makes recipes and learning pages easy to find.</figcaption>
</figure>

**Wireframing & prototypes** — Low- and mid-fidelity wireframes in Axure and Sketch, validated in stakeholder sessions.

**Collaboration** — Partnered with creative, design, and development teams, documenting IA guidelines and mentoring junior designers.

## Outcomes

- A restructured, accessible website architecture supporting the brand vision
- Better content findability through cleaner taxonomy and simplified navigation
- A scalable IA foundation for future growth
- Improved mobile engagement and accessibility compliance
