## Overview

Modernization of TD Insurance's online quoter tool serving thousands of travelers. The redesign aimed to enhance conversion rates, reduce customer confusion, and achieve regulatory accessibility compliance — without sacrificing the clarity and trust that high-stakes insurance decisions require.

## The Challenge

The legacy interface was cluttered and text-heavy. Users abandoned mid-quote due to unclear steps, jargon, and inconsistent error handling. The experience failed people at the exact moment they needed the most clarity.

## Approach

Lead UX Designer role encompassing the full engagement:

- Discovery research and competitive benchmarking
- Stakeholder co-design workshops
- Information architecture redesign
- High-fidelity prototyping in Figma and Sketch
- Plain-language content strategy to replace insurance jargon
- Design system expansion with accessible, reusable components
- Usability testing with assistive-technology users

## Outcomes

- Form-completion improved by **18%**
- User satisfaction rose **22%** post-launch
- Achieved AODA/WCAG 2.1 AA certification across web and mobile
- Influenced TD's accessibility playbook and internal training modules
