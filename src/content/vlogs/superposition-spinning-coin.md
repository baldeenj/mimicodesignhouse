## Show Notes

Quantum superposition is one of the most misunderstood concepts in computing. This episode uses the accessible analogy of a spinning coin to make it concrete.

**Topics covered:**

- What superposition means in quantum mechanics
- How qubits differ from classical bits (existing in multiple states simultaneously)
- The spinning coin as an instructional tool
- Superposition's role in quantum algorithms
- Why measurement collapses quantum states
- The balance between quantum computers' power and fragility
- Quantum computing is about carefully controlling quantum states so the right answers become more likely when measured — not about running things in parallel
