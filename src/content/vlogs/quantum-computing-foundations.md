## Show Notes

A rigorous but accessible grounding in quantum computing fundamentals. Addresses common misconceptions and explains why quantum computers won't replace classical systems but extend computational capabilities beyond classical limits.

**Topics covered:**

- Why quantum computing exists in the first place
- Qubits vs. classical bits
- Superposition and interference in computation
- Entanglement's role in system scaling
- Decoherence — quantum computing's primary engineering challenge
- Engineering requirements for quantum systems
- Practical applications and where quantum computing actually helps
