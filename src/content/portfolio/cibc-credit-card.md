*Role: Lead User Experience Designer — CIBC (Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce).*

## Overview

CIBC's credit-card application suffered from outdated design and poor performance. The redesign focused on raising completion rates, improving accessibility, and aligning with enterprise standards — all while managing complex regulatory requirements.

## The Challenge

- High mobile and mid-application abandonment
- WCAG 2.1 AA gaps in forms and interactions
- Complex regulatory disclosures creating friction
- Missing status feedback and error messaging
- Patterns inconsistent with CIBC's other banking applications

## Our Approach

Discovery research, journey mapping, and progressive disclosure shaped a flow of **five intuitive stages** — card selection through terms acceptance — with real-time validation, responsive layouts, accessible controls, and plain-language microcopy. Close collaboration with accessibility and compliance teams ensured AODA / WCAG adherence.

  <img src="/uploads/cibc-cc-2.png" alt="Card selection and comparison step of the application." />
  <figcaption>Plain-language guidance and clear feedback turn a dense, regulated form into a confident, guided flow.</figcaption>
</figure>

## Outcomes

- **32%** increase in application-completion rate
- **28%** reduction in form abandonment
- Full WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Reusable patterns applied to loans and other credit products
- Greater customer trust through improved transparency
